How barometric pressure moves elk

Ask an old-timer why the elk "turned off" and you will often hear one word: pressure. Not hunting pressure, barometric pressure. Here is the field wisdom, and how to use it.

Elk are built to read the weather before it arrives. A falling barometer signals an incoming front, and elk tend to feed hard and move ahead of it, stocking up before they hunker down. A sharp rise behind a front often brings a burst of movement too, especially the first clear, cold morning after a storm clears out.

The windows that matter

Steady high pressure and warm afternoons are the flat stretches. Elk bed early, move little, and hold in the dark timber. The productive windows cluster around change: the hours before a front, and the cold, clearing morning after one. That is when a daily movement forecast earns its keep.
